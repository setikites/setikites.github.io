Folkboat Electric

Our folkboat relies heavily on an automatic bilge pump to stay afloat. This is powered from a single lead acid 12VDC battery. Charge on that battery is maintained by solar panels.

Current Wiring

Wiring is competent but less than professional. The entire system includes DC and AC circuits. DC components include solar panels, solar charger, battery, lights, switches, fuses, bilge pump, radio and DC outlets. AC components include inverter, switches, and AC outlets.

Solar Panels

Two monocrystalline panels, each with 24 cells, mounted to aft deck. These may be BlueSolar 30W panels at max 18 VDC max 1.67A. (pic) (data sheet)

Junction box says NBJH PV-JH03 1000V which is for Ningbo Jinghua Optics solar panel junction box. This connector is reated for 600V and 5.5A.

Solar Controller

Morningstar SunSaver-10 solar controller SS-10-12V (pic) (manual)

This charger supports maximum 30V 10A solar, 12V 10A system load. PWM charging uses four stages: night, bulk charge 15V max, absorption 14.4V, float 13.7V, and equalize 14.9V. LEDs provide system status and battery status (voltage) indicating which charging stage applies.

Planned Wiring

Must Have

Updates will include bringing all wiring up to ABYC marine standard:

  • Check wire sizes
  • Replace all connections with ring terminals
  • Bus bars
  • Terminal strips or fuse blocks
  • Make sure all circuits start with fuses. Fuse any time wire down-sizes.
  • Battery box must be secured with aluminum angle and ratchet straps

May Add

Additional components may include:

  • Battery / system status display
  • Upgrade to MPPT solar charger per panel
  • Bilge pump counter $50

May Remove

Simplifying may include removing VAC circuit along with the inverter. If keep inverter, make sure there is a DC disconnect switch before it.

Calculations

Solar Sizing

  • Maine gets 4.2 hours of peak sunlight per day
  • DC solar system size(W) = Daily load (Wh) / 4.2 hours * 1.15 efficiency
  • Assume 60 W / 1.5 * 4.2 hours = 168 Wh per day

Bilge Pump Sizing

  • 1500 Gallons/hour pump draws 7A at 13.6VDC (95 watts)
  • 1500 gph delivers 1200 gph with 1 meter rise
  • constant operation is 95 watts x 24 hours or 2,300 watt hours
  • 168 Wh at 95 watts = 1.7 hours
  • 1200 gph at 1.7 hours = 2,122 gallons per day

Bilge Pump Sizing

  • 4000 Gallons/hour pump draws 20A at 13.6VDC (272 watts)
  • 4000 gph delivers 3500 gph with 1 meter rise
  • constant operation is 272 watts x 24 hours or 6,528 watt hours
  • 168 Wh at 272 watts = 0.6 hours
  • 3500 gph at 0.6 hours = 2,161 gallons per day
